## Competitive Landscape / Market Foils

### Blue Owl Capital
- **What it is:** Large alternative asset manager (~$18B market cap, pre-crisis) focused on private credit — primarily direct lending to software and tech companies, plus GP stakes
- **The crisis (Feb 2026):** Stock down ~60% in 13 months; halted redemptions on OBDC II ($1.6B fund), telling investors it will wind down and return capital on an unspecified timeline rather than honoring quarterly withdrawal windows. Sold $1.4B in loans at 99.8% of carrying value — but some sales were to affiliated entities, raising questions about cherry-picking
- **Why it matters (headlines):** Bloomberg, CNBC, CNN, Yahoo Finance all running major stories; Treasury Secretary Bessent said he's "concerned" about systemic risk; Sen. Warren called them "a shadowy private credit firm"; Mohamed El-Erian invoked Bear Stearns 2007 "canary in the coal mine" framing; Deutsche Bank downgraded to Hold, cut PT from $15 → $10
- **AREC contrast (key talking points):**
  - AREC lends against **residential land with homebuilder demand** — not software companies with no hard collateral
  - **A&D loans are unlevered** — no leverage on 80% of capital; no gating risk
  - **No retail investors** — institutional LP structure only; no quarterly redemption promises to retail
  - **Zero credit losses in Fund I** — 14.3% net returns, fully realized
  - **Defined timelines** — loans are short-duration (18–36 months), not open-ended credit facilities
  - AREC's borrowers are **top-10 national builders** (Lennar, DR Horton, etc.) with existing takeout demand — not speculative tech lenders
- **Use case:** Position AREC as the clean, transparent, hard-collateral alternative when LPs are questioning private credit broadly
