# Fund Structure Legal Review — Belgravia/DAF GP Operating Agreement

**Date:** 2026-02-23
**Source:** [Notion](https://www.notion.so/3108c54f8b2c80538728de77ed4cda65)
**Attendees:** Oscar Vasquez, Mike Righetti, Mike Wigrizer (Belgravia), Don (legal), Varsha (legal), AREC team

## Summary

A detailed legal review of the GP Operating Agreement for the Belgravia/DAF investment into Fund II. The call covered six substantive issues and resolved most of them, with the goal of closing this week — this agreement is the last item blocking the next fund close.

On the target vertical commitment definition, the team confirmed it will always be 20% of aggregate capital commitments and removed redundant "lesser of" language. The distribution mechanics discussion (Section 8.1a) was the most complex — pre-true-up distributions will be based on the greater of 20% or pro rata allocation to horizontal development, which minimizes future true-up amounts. The DAF can only be under-distributed (never over-distributed), so risk is one-directional. Mike Wigrizer requested a clawback provision as a safeguard even though it's unlikely to be needed, and the team agreed.

On emergency expenses (Section 6.1), Belgravia took a firm position that LP commitment must be an absolute cap — no additional capital calls above commitment, full stop. This is a hard line across all Belgravia deals. The solution: allow other members to fund emergency expenses via a loan with non-punitive interest rates and a right of first offer to all members. AREC noted their 10% cash buffer on commitments should cover most scenarios.

Tony's DAF commitment structure: the DAF will commit up to $10M, matched one-for-one with Tony's personal/family commitment (through a single family trust). The team agreed to use a separate representation/wrap rather than attaching estate planning documents to the operating agreement. Oscar needs to confirm with Ira whether the $5M or $10M commitment threshold applies given the fund will be at ~$250M (not $350M as originally contemplated).

Annual company expenses reduced from $125K to $50K. Confidentiality provisions need work — the carry member was exempted but this creates a gap. Solution: add language that employees are bound by separate confidentiality provisions.

The LLC for the DAF investment was expected to be set up the following morning, with UBS handling banking. This is the only remaining item preventing the close.

## Key Decisions

- Target vertical commitment = 20% of aggregate capital commitments (simplified)
- Clawback provision added as safeguard for distribution true-up
- Emergency funding via member loan (non-punitive), not additional capital calls
- Tony's commitment verified via separate rep/wrap, not estate planning document disclosure
- Annual company expenses reduced from $125K to $50K
- Carry member confidentiality gap to be addressed
