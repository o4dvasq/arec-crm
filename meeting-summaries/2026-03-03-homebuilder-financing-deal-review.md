# Homebuilder Financing Deal Review

**Date:** 2026-03-03
**Source:** [Notion](https://www.notion.so/3188c54f8b2c80958362e339a707e2bf)
**Attendees:** Tony Avila, Oscar Vasquez, Patrick Fichtner, Nate Cichon, Zach Reisner, Glen, John, Drew, Philip, Marc, Jim, Michelle

## Summary

Nate presented two new vertical (construction) lending opportunities for the fund. Both deals fit fund parameters and represent quality additions to the portfolio.

**Pressboro Homes (Charlotte)** — A $16M facility at prime+1% with 1 point in fees. Pressboro does ~200 homes annually, ranking ~#31 in Charlotte (top 10 among private builders). Financials are steady with gross margins of 19-24% and net margins above 10% in strong years. The main watch item is spec concentration: 75% of inventory is spec-focused with ~35 completed infill specs in Charlotte representing about half of total spec. However, months of supply is manageable at ~5 months based on sales. The builder is expanding from Charlotte into South Carolina, Atlanta (~50 units/year), Marietta, and Savannah. IRR expected to hurdle on the higher side vs. portfolio. Patrick noted the team targets top 20 private builders in any major market, not just top 200 nationally.

**Chris Brown Homes (Fort Myers)** — A $30M facility at prime+0.75% with 75bps annual facility fee ($75K) plus $24K servicing and $150/draw. Ranked #94 nationally, strong top-100 builder doing 500+ homes/year with $200M+ revenue. 50% owned by Barron Collier Companies, which controls 65,000+ acres in Florida — a significant strategic land pipeline advantage. Balance sheet is solid ($47M net worth, 1.2x leverage, $31M cash). The primary risk is margin compression in Southwest Florida (currently 2.7% net, targeting 5% in 2026). Mitigants include geographic diversification beyond SW Florida, product mix shift to higher-margin move-up homes, exiting underperforming communities (Babcock Ranch, Westport), and aggressive cost management. The facility fee structure is a first for AREC's vertical lending program, providing upfront revenue diversification. Team consensus: keep moving on both deals.

## Key Decisions

- Both Pressboro Homes and Chris Brown Homes approved to continue through underwriting
- Pressboro: spec covenant set at 50% high watermark, consistent with lender requirements
- Chris Brown: 4:1 lot-to-spec ratio beginning 12 months from closing; 7-month spec supply covenant based on trailing 12
- Chris Brown: facility fee structure (first for AREC) vs. per-unit fee model
