# Family Office Partnership Discussion

**Date:** 2026-02-24
**Source:** [Notion](https://www.notion.so/3118c54f8b2c804b8fffe6482a6236b9)
**Attendees:** Oscar Vasquez (presenting as AREC), Anthony (from the family office group)

## Summary

Oscar took a pitch call from a family office intermediary group — a UK-headquartered firm (est. 1983) that works exclusively with single family offices, multifamily offices, and RIAs with minimum $150M AUM (average client $650M). They claim relationships with 2,300 family offices globally (1,800 domestic) and have facilitated over $20B in capital deployment over three decades.

The group is running a mandate to source a new real estate debt offering for their allocating families. They're evaluating 18 firms, with 3 moving forward to their annual Investment Committee — competing firms include Trans Capital (Fund 9), Arden (Fund 6), Lone Star (Fund 4), Walton Asset (Land Finance Fund 3), and Invictus Real Estate Partners.

Oscar gave a thorough walkthrough of AREC's background (Encore origins, transition to lending, Fund I track record of $260M with $1.3B originated, no defaults, 14.3% realized return) and Fund II details ($1B target, 14-16% net returns, first mortgage lending to private homebuilders, REIT structure with tax benefits).

Anthony acknowledged the strategy looks strong but flagged the timing challenge — Fund II final close is June 30th, which likely precludes their IC process for this fund. However, he was open to positioning for Fund III.

The economics: $110,000 flat fee to the GP if they make it through the IC. No back-end fees, no percentage of raise. The process involves an internal press release to allocating families, an RFP platform where family offices post live mandates, and formal one-on-one sit-downs in Boston (June) and Dallas (September) with 75-80 families attending.

Oscar's closing: "Let me and Anthony go back and discuss that figure internally, and then we'll get back."

## Key Decisions

- No commitment made — Oscar to discuss the $110K fee with Tony internally
- Timing likely pushes this to a Fund III opportunity
- Both sides agreed the strategy/product is a good fit for family office investors
