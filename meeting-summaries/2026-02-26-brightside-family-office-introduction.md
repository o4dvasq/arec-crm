# Brightside Family Office Introduction Call

**Date:** 2026-02-26
**Source:** [Notion](https://www.notion.so/3138c54f8b2c8028b610fa6199c4ccfe)
**Attendees:** Tony Avila, Oscar Vasquez, Anthony Albuquerque, Patrick (Brightside Family Office)

## Summary

Introductory pitch call with Patrick from Brightside, the single family office of the Newhall family. The Newhalls are a venture dynasty — Chuck Newhall founded NEA (New Enterprise Associates) in the late 1970s, and his son Ashton founded Greenspring in 2000 (venture fund of funds), sold it to StepStone in 2021, then launched the single family office in late 2024. The family has heavy venture/growth equity exposure with additional allocations to real estate, private credit, and PE.

Patrick joined Brightside about a year ago from a commercial real estate background — previously at JLL in San Francisco (debt/equity placement) and Argosy. He's based in the West Palm Beach area with the family. Return targets: 2x net on equity, "cover burn" strategy on credit. Very tax-focused as a single family office.

Tony delivered the standard Fund II pitch covering the full capital stack structure (A&D + vertical), mortgage REIT tax efficiency (20% discount off ordinary income), 13% current yield / 15% net IRR, and the 80/20 unleveraged/leveraged split. Patrick asked good questions about sourcing, geographic focus, market sensitivity, and insurance/climate risk. Tony emphasized the competitive moat — in-house development staff, iBank relationships through BAG, and builder LP network providing both deal validation and backup buyers.

Oscar covered the investment banking angle through BAG (3 of 20 current public builder IPOs led by the team) and the vertical integration providing both alpha generation and downside protection. Patrick was engaged but had to drop early. Noted this was his first exposure to the for-sale/horizontal development side — JLL and Argosy didn't touch it. He expressed interest in mentioning the opportunity to the Newhall family members.
