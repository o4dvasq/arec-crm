# RXR/AREC Venture Term Sheet Review

**Date:** 2026-02-25
**Source:** [Notion](https://www.notion.so/3128c54f8b2c8081922cc9f3fa609953)
**Attendees:** Tony Avila, Oscar Vasquez, Zach Reisner, Mike Righetti (GC), Steve, Jeannie (Clifford Chance), Felicia (Clifford Chance)

## Summary

The team walked through Felicia's list of key open items on the RXR venture term sheet, with the goal of getting to execution this week. The overall tone was positive — the two sides are close, with only a handful of substantive issues remaining. There's a call with RXR scheduled for tomorrow afternoon to resolve the open items verbally before turning a final draft.

The biggest structural issues are around exit/liquidity rights and exclusivity. Tony doesn't want a buy-sell mechanism (RXR can commit up to $1B, making it impractical for AREC to match), and instead wants a put right or secondary transfer mechanism — especially important for transitioning positions from Fund II to a future Fund III. The team also flagged that RXR shouldn't be able to indefinitely extend exclusivity by unilaterally upsizing capital commitments while simultaneously rejecting deals. Tony suggested a "three strikes" or time-based policy to prevent that.

On fees, RXR had assumed AREC's 10% co-invest would be subject to fees — AREC said no. Tony's position: don't kill the deal over ~$1.4M over seven years. Let them choose between market-rate rent or more carry, and negotiate in the legal docs. The desk leasing item was taken off the term sheet entirely.

The second half of the meeting shifted to the Nomura repo line discussion. The team reviewed Nomura's latest response, which was described as a "bummer" — they're qualifying everything and pushing for recourse to the fund beyond true bad-boy acts. Tony and Oscar are concerned this is a counterparty that will try to find technical foot-faults to push losses back to the fund if anything goes wrong. The consensus is Tony needs to talk to Todd at Hillwood to gauge whether Hillwood (and DR Horton) would even be comfortable with Nomura's posture before spending more legal dollars. Jeannie will respond to Nomura that AREC is socializing internally and will follow up.

## Key Decisions

- Exit mechanism should NOT be a buy-sell; prefer put right or secondary transfer to Fund III
- Desk leasing/licensing removed from term sheet — will figure out market rent later
- Fee dispute (10% co-invest being fee'd) to be negotiated in legal docs, not term sheet
- Nomura: pause further turns until Tony talks to Todd at Hillwood about comfort level
- Non-poaching clause to be drafted in legal docs (mutual), not term sheet
