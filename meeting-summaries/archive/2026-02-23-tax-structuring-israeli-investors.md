# Tax Structuring for Israeli Investors

**Date:** 2026-02-23
**Source:** [Notion](https://www.notion.so/3108c54f8b2c80149c4df01768d50605)
**Attendees:** Oscar Vasquez, Tony Avila, Lior, Eitan/Arik (Israeli tax advisors), AREC team

## Summary

A technical deep-dive on how to structure Fund II for Israeli institutional investors. The current REIT structure works well for U.S. investors (20% tax reduction) but creates problems for Israeli institutions because REIT dividends are unfavorable from an Israeli tax perspective. Israeli investors are extremely sensitive to tax leakage and will compare funds on after-tax returns.

The tax advisors proposed a parallel leveraged REIT structure utilizing the portfolio interest exemption — a well-established approach used by Madison, Hillwood, and other major fund managers. The key mechanism: loans from foreign residents to U.S. corporations qualify for the portfolio interest exemption, provided no single investor exceeds 10% ownership. The 10% threshold is based only on voting rights, not equity, which enables a "low-high vote" structure where institutional investors hold only profit rights while the GP maintains voting control. Israeli insurance companies have multiple segments (each considered a separate taxpayer), making the 10% threshold manageable.

The team's previously investigated Cayman corporate feeder was addressed — the advisors said it doesn't mitigate Israeli institutional taxation. However, a Cayman structure may still be relevant for private Israeli individuals (through a partnership checked as corporation, allowing tax credits for REIT taxes paid).

Cost analysis: $200-300K in legal fees to set up, $50-75K annually for fund admin/audit/tax prep (tax advisor estimated closer to $100K/year ongoing). To avoid materially decreasing investor returns, the team needs north of $150-200M in aggregate Israeli commitments.

The strategic consensus: Fund II's June 30th close makes it tight but not impossible. The practical path is likely a first trip to Israel in March/April to build relationships and gauge tangible interest, then return in November with deployment track record to secure anchor investors for Fund III. Nobody wants to spend $200-300K on restructuring without confirmed investor interest.

## Key Decisions

- Parallel leveraged REIT with low-high vote structure is the recommended approach for Israeli institutions
- Cayman feeder does NOT mitigate Israeli institutional taxation (may work for private individuals)
- Won't incur restructuring expenses without confirmed investor interest
- Likely a Fund III play, with groundwork laid now
