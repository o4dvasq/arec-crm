# RXR & Nomura — Internal Strategy Prep

**Date:** 2026-02-25
**Source:** [Notion](https://www.notion.so/3128c54f8b2c8081922cc9f3fa609953)
**Attendees:** Oscar Vasquez, Zach Reisner, Mike Righetti, Felicia, Jeannie Roig-Irwin (Clifford Chance)

## Summary

Internal strategy session preparing for the next day's RXR term sheet call, plus a discussion of Nomura facility concerns. This was a working session to align positions before the Feb 26 negotiation.

**RXR Term Sheet Prep:** The team walked through Felicia's list of key open items. On **upsizing/exclusivity**, the concern is that RXR could effectively extend exclusivity indefinitely by unilaterally increasing capital commitments — since exclusivity only lapses once capital is fully funded. Zach proposed a "three strikes" or time-based mechanism to prevent this. The team agreed to raise it directionally on the call without pushing for specific term sheet language yet. On **liquidity/exit**, consensus was that a buy-sell doesn't work given RXR's potential $1B commitment vs. AREC's smaller position. Preferred alternatives: put right, secondary transfer to a successor fund, or fund-to-fund rollover. On **governance**, RXR wants some unilateral decision rights (unspecified) — team agreed to ask for specifics on the call. On **fees**, RXR's model assumed AREC's 10% equity would be fee-bearing; AREC said no. Resolution likely through carry adjustment. Tony's office desk arrangement removed from term sheet entirely — team pushed to handle separately after closing. On **existing loans**, team doesn't believe there's a real disagreement — just a drafting point to clarify.

**Nomura Facility:** The team reviewed Nomura's latest response to the repo facility terms and the tone was discouraging. Nomura is qualifying everything and pushing for recourse to the fund on what AREC considers foot faults — not just bad boy acts. Jeannie and the team flagged that Nomura has historically sharp elbows and would use any technicality to push losses back to the fund in a downside scenario. Oscar recommended Tony talk to Todd (Hillwood) and DR Horton to gauge LP comfort before spending more legal dollars. The team's instinct is to let Hillwood potentially kill it rather than keep negotiating. Mike noted a previous Nomura facility where they spent $1.5M on legal and Nomura never funded a loan. Jeannie to respond that the team is "socializing internally."

Side items: Team agreed DR Horton's sub-docs don't need heavy scrutiny (public company, obvious qualified purchaser). Non-poach clause to be added in legal docs (not term sheet). Mutual exclusivity for RXR rejected as unrealistic.

## Key Decisions

- Buy-sell mechanism rejected for RXR exit — prefer put right or fund-to-fund transfer
- Office/desk arrangement removed from RXR term sheet
- Nomura: pause further negotiation until Tony socializes with Hillwood/DR Horton
- DR Horton sub-doc scrutiny level: minimal (public company)
