# RXR Term Sheet Negotiation

**Date:** 2026-02-26
**Source:** [Notion](https://www.notion.so/3138c54f8b2c80629591d341934430a7)
**Attendees:** Zach Reisner, Mike Righetti, Jeannie Roig-Irwin (Clifford Chance), Steven Fields (RXR), David Kelly (RXR), Brian (RXR), Felicia

## Summary

Near-final term sheet negotiation call between AREC and RXR for the vertical construction lending venture. Parties are very close to signing. Key points resolved or discussed:

**Upsizing:** RXR confirmed no punitive dilution if they upsize the venture to $1B without AREC approval — just one-to-one dilution. Debt fund commitment: up to 20% of fund size (so up to $200M for a $1B fund).

**Liquidity/Term:** If the venture outlives the debt fund, AREC wants the right to exit or transfer interest to a successor fund. Both sides agreed buy-sell doesn't work. Liquidity rights punted to documentation phase.

**Governance:** RXR wants certain unilateral decision rights as the 90% partner — primarily around exercising remedies (default interest, selling loans) and liquidity decisions, not day-to-day operations. Significant unresolved issue: AREC's LPs require LPAC or anchor investor approval before the venture can foreclose and take title to real estate. RXR concerned about being blocked from exercising remedies by a small LP blocking right. This needs further work.

**Fees/Carry:** Agreed on 60-40 or 55-45 carry split for AREC's 10% equity. Fee credits on the 2% venture fee split 50-50, with AREC crediting their share on the carry side. Invested capital basis confirmed.

**Exclusivity:** Existing vertical loans not transferred can stay in AREC's fund. Exclusivity applies to new opportunities going forward after closing.

**Office:** Removed from term sheet — will be handled separately via desk licensing.

## Key Decisions

- No punitive dilution on upsize — one-to-one only
- Buy-sell mechanism rejected; liquidity rights punted to docs
- Office arrangements removed from term sheet
- Fee structure agreed: invested capital basis, carry credit for fee split
