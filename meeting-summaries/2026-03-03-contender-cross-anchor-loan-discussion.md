# Contender Cross Anchor Loan Discussion

**Date:** 2026-03-03

**Attendees:** Tony Avila, Oscar Vasquez, Glen (Ops), Mike Righetti (GC), Patrick Fichtner, John, Drew, Marc, Nate

## Summary

The team convened to assess the Contender Cross Anchor A&D loan opportunity against the backdrop of ongoing SEC litigation and a new class action lawsuit alleging fraudulent conveyance and Ponzi scheme activity against the borrower. While the underlying deal fundamentals are solid — good land in the right location, will-serves obtained, Lennar confirmed as takedown buyer — the team expressed significant trust and transparency concerns with Contender as a borrower.

Tony made the case for proceeding, citing capital deployment pressure: Fund II has deployed only $13M since November despite targeting $1B AUM, with Mountain House not closing until June/July/August. His proposed risk mitigation strategy centers on aggressive legal protections — strict line-item budget controls, covenant triggers for misrepresentation, cross-collateralization with the existing Catawba deal, and reduced borrower equity return (already cut from $11-12M to $7.5M).

The team also discussed the related Catawba deal, where maturity is approaching and Contender is having trouble finding replacement lenders. Fund I's investment period expires June 27, so any refinance would be structured as a new loan rather than an extension. Tony noted the opportunistic angle: if Contender faces liquidity challenges, being a performing lender gives AREC leverage for additional opportunities. Worst-case exit involves a JV structure to take assets out of the fund.

Mike Righetti has an email ready for Clifford Chance's regulatory counsel to reassess risk in light of the new class action complaint. The key legal question: could a court finding of fraudulent conveyance vacate AREC's first-position lien? Tony believes this is unlikely but wants formal legal confirmation.

An interesting market backdrop emerged: Gulf region instability over the past 60 hours has triggered significant capital flight from Middle Eastern family offices seeking stable US hard-asset investments — very receptive to AREC's strategy.

## Key Decisions

- Proceed with further diligence on Cross Anchor, contingent on Clifford Chance legal opinion
- Pursue cross-collateralization with Catawba deal (mechanics TBD given Fund 1 vs Fund 2 structure)
- Reduce borrower equity and increase fee protections
- Nail down all budget line items with strict covenants — no ability to move between items
